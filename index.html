The story that started everything

Sid Oakley built Cedar Creek Gallery in Creedmoor, NC over a lifetime. Artists. Potters. Painters. People who made things with their hands. He built a home for them — and they built a community around him.

When Sid died, the people who loved him filled a church.

The preacher walked to the lectern. But instead of speaking, he reached down and placed a small cassette player on the edge of the pulpit. Bent the microphone toward it. And pressed play.

The room went quiet.

Then:

"Well, I guess I am the last person you all expected to hear from today."

Everybody laughed. And then everybody cried. Because it was Sid — his voice, his timing, his particular gift for walking into a room and making it feel like home.

He had recorded it himself. Before he died. He knew his people would need to hear him one more time.

Afterwords is built so everyone can do what Sid did.
