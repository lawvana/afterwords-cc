The story that started this

Sid Oakley built Cedar Creek Gallery in Creedmoor, NC over a lifetime. When he died, the people who loved him filled a church.

The preacher walked to the lectern — but instead of speaking, he bent the microphone down toward a small cassette player sitting on the edge of the pulpit. And pressed play.

The room went quiet.

"Well, I guess I am the last person
you all expected to hear from today."

Everybody laughed. And then everybody cried. Because it was Sid — his voice, his timing, his particular gift for walking into a room and making it feel like home.

He had recorded it himself. He knew his people would need to hear him one more time.

Afterwords is built so everyone can do what Sid did.
